## Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)
Identify the vital few factors driving the majority of outcomes.
In business: 20% of products often generate 80% of revenue.
Calculate cumulative percentages to find the "vital few" threshold.

## Growth Pattern Analysis
Types of growth to identify:
- **Linear Growth**: Steady, constant-rate increase
- **Exponential Growth**: Accelerating increase (percentage stays constant)
- **Plateau**: Growth slowing after an initial rapid period
- **Seasonal**: Recurring patterns tied to time periods (quarters, months)

## Gap Analysis Framework
1. Define the current state (as-is)
2. Define the desired state (to-be)
3. Identify the gap (difference)
4. Prioritize gaps by business impact
5. Recommend actions to close high-priority gaps

## Anomaly Narration Pattern
Transform statistical outliers into business narratives:
1. State the anomaly (what, when, where)
2. Quantify the deviation (how far from normal)
3. Propose business explanation (why might this have happened)
4. Assess impact (positive or negative signal)

## SWOT (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
Classic business framework for strategic analysis.
- Strengths & Weaknesses: Internal factors
- Opportunities & Threats: External factors
